Cats in North Africa

Many argue that the ancient Egyptians were the first to tame and domesticate cats, but there is no general opinion on this matter.
It is believed that the history of the appearance of cats next to a person begins about 10-12 thousand years ago and is connected with the fact that these animals have become close to people since the time when the latter began to lead a sedentary lifestyle. This, by the way, makes cats simply unique among all animals, many of which still try to stay away from people.

A lot of controversy is also caused by the history of the origin of cats, which is also very ambiguous. The most common opinion, which, by the way, especially took root in Russia, is that the only ancestor of modern cats is the Western Asian North African steppe cat, which was domesticated not in Egypt at all, but in Nubia, and this happened about 4 thousand years ago. Only after that, cats came to Egypt, and then appeared in Asia, where they successfully interbred with forest Bengal species.

Cats in Egypt

In ancient Egypt, cats were very popular pets. By exterminating rodents, they protected crops and saved people from epidemics, primarily plague. They also hunted vipers, of which there were many in Egypt, and could even participate in bird hunting or fishing, that is, they were used as hunting animals, like modern dogs. Sacred Egyptian cat. In general, cats were considered sacred animals, associating them with various gods. So, it was believed that the supreme Egyptian god Ra took on the appearance of a red cat when he fought the serpent Apep in the kingdom of the dead. And cat-Ra defeated the snake, as Egyptian cats defeated African vipers! And in Egypt they worshiped Bast, or Bastet, the goddess of joy, love and fertility, who was depicted with a female body and a cat's head. Bast was considered the daughter of the sun god Ra and the moon goddess Hathor. Merciful, supportive of people, Bast wore a cat's head, and the angry goddess was portrayed with a lion's head. The lioness-Bast brought death to the enemies. A whole city, Bubastis, was dedicated to the goddess and her sacred cats. A magnificent temple was built there, and crowded holidays were held. Egyptian goddess Bastet. It is not surprising that the Egyptians were very respectful towards animals dedicated to the great gods. At every temple where cats lived, a "guardian of cats" served, and this important position was inherited. For killing a cat, even unintentionally, the perpetrator could pay with his life. When a cat died in the house, all household members shaved their eyebrows as a sign of mourning, and the body of the dead cat was mummified, that is, they kept it from decomposition with the help of a special treatment - embalming. Mourning was worn for 70 days, so much time was usually taken by mummification. There were special Egyptian cemeteries where the burials of cat mummies were located. Abyssinian cat. Many cat mummies have survived to this day, with their help zoologists have restored the appearance of the most common pets in Egypt. They were medium-sized cats, lean, reddish, with a uniform coat color. Most of all modern cats look like representatives of the Abyssinian breed, whose ancestors also come from Africa. The Egyptians forbade the export of cats outside the borders of their state. But when Egypt was conquered by Rome in 30 BC, the cats, taken out by the victorious Romans, quickly spread throughout Europe and Asia. The Egyptians used cats as hunting animals. In one ancient Egyptian fresco, a cat scares away birds in the Nile swamps. The fresco is almost 3500 years old and was found in ancient Thebes (the modern city of Luxor).

But how did cats come to be successful?

To answer this question, we will have to go back 66 million years ago, to a time when there were still several million years before the appearance of felines (Feliformia). In those days, the Miacidae family appeared, whose representatives were supposedly the ancestors of the carnivores (Carnivora). And the first Carnivora were the ancestors of the cats (Feliformia) and dogs (Caniformia). Members of the Miacidae family were small carnivores who ran through the trees and hunted for medium-sized prey. They presumably preferred insects, lizards, and small birds. A typical representative of this family is Dormaalocyon latouri, who lived about 56-47 million years ago. Its weight did not exceed one kilogram. This animal is one of the few known to science that can be classified as a common ancestor (or close to a common ancestor) of dogs and cats. Unfortunately, there is not much data on this period of cat evolution. And the reason for this lies in the habitat of these animals. They lived in the forest, and this area (despite the abundance of living organisms) is not suitable for fossilization (formation of fossils). After all, animals dying in the forest can be immediately eaten by other animals. And if no one found the corpse, then it will rot without it. So that the skeleton simply does not have time

History of the Siberian cat - Russia.

For the first time, cats similar to Siberian ones are mentioned in publications of the 17th century about the city of Kansk, Krasnoyarsk district, Yenisei province. Then they were called "Bukhara". They could be found everywhere in the Russian kingdom. There is no exact data on when and how Bukhara cats appeared in Siberia. At that time, the indigenous inhabitants of Siberia led a predominantly nomadic lifestyle. But there were also large camps (settlements) in Siberia - the headquarters of the khans, where the settled population kept livestock, cats and dogs. During the colonization of Siberia by Russian settlers, they brought with them domestic animals and cats to fight rodents. Bukhara cats came to Siberia with merchants who arrived from the Central Asian countries, with whom there was an active trade at that time. It is possible that Angora, Siberian and Persian cats had common Asian ancestors. Russian merchants from Veliky Ustyug and Lalsk (Vologda province), who were engaged in the XVI-XVII centuries. various trade, along with the goods they brought home long-haired Siberian cats, which took root there. The Siberian cat as a breed was formed on the territory of the Urals and east of the Urals - in Siberia. Severe weather conditions, an abundance of snow, severe frosts and winds contributed to the appearance of a long dense coat and thick undercoat in the Siberian cat, protecting the animal from the cold. Siberian cats are widespread in the north of the European part of Russia, in the central and western regions of the country.

American Wirehair

The first rough-haired kitten was born on a farm in New York State in 1966. The kitten, which appeared as a result of a spontaneous mutation, was bought by a local breeder of American Shorthair cats, Joan O'Shea, who wanted to breed a wire-haired breed. It soon became clear that the mutation is dominant and it is not difficult to breed such cats. When a rough-haired cat is crossed with a regular short-haired cat, there will always be a few rough-haired kittens in the litter.[1] The first cat was named Adam, as it was the progenitor, as was the biblical character of the same name, the full official name of the ancestor of the Council Rock Farm Adam of Hi-Fi breed. Joan O'Shea also bought two more rough-haired kittens, whom she named Abby and Emmy. Emmy was later acquired by Bill and Madeline Beck, who bred wavy-coated cats. Wool samples from the offspring of this cat were sent for examination to confirm genetic uniqueness.[2] In the future, it was decided to use crossbreeding with the American smooth-haired to form a new breed. Among the descendants of Emmy was the cat Barberry Ellen, who became the first homozygous cat with a "wire" coat. O'Shea stopped working with the breed in 1970, the Beck family a little later, other breeders continued to work with the breed. At the moment, the American Wirehair breed is considered one of the rarest in the world.
